About

A project-based home for interdisciplinary research.

The Religious Cognition Collaborative develops and coordinates research on religion as a cognitive, psychological, neural, social, and cultural phenomenon. We connect investigators across institutions and fields, build teams around clearly defined questions, and provide continuity from initial design through analysis, publication, and public communication.

Why RCC exists

Why RCC exists

Religious cognition crosses conventional disciplinary boundaries. Questions about belief, experience, ritual, agency, development, and culture often require more than one method and more than one kind of expertise. RCC exists to give those questions an organizational home and to make sustained collaboration easier.

How we work

How we work

Some projects begin within RCC. Others begin with a collaborator, an open dataset, a funding opportunity, or a problem that requires a broader team. RCC helps refine the question, define roles, identify the necessary expertise, coordinate the work, and move the project toward a concrete output.

Open inquiry

Open inquiry across traditions

RCC is nonconfessional. We study religion without presuming either its truth or falsity, and we welcome researchers from religious, nonreligious, and philosophically diverse backgrounds. No single religious tradition is treated as the default model of religion when broader comparison is possible.

Organizational home

Organizational and fiscal home

The Religious Cognition Collaborative is a U.S. 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization and operates under that registered name. It provides fiscal and administrative oversight for the research it develops and coordinates.

Research standards

How we hold the work to account.

Questions before methods

Projects begin with a defined research question and use the methods best suited to answer it.

Claims proportionate to evidence

We distinguish exploratory, directional, null, and confirmatory findings and avoid overstating what a design can establish.

Transparent roles and credit

Leadership, authorship, and public credit should reflect actual responsibility and contribution.

Responsible open science

We support reproducible analysis, transparent reporting, and the responsible sharing of data and materials where consent, ethics, and law permit.
