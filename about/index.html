About

About the Collaborative

The Religious Cognition Collaborative is an interdisciplinary research organization devoted to the rigorous study of religious cognition. We connect researchers across institutions and fields to develop focused projects, combine complementary expertise, and produce scholarship that is both methodologically strong and intellectually open.

Mission

Our Mission

Our mission is to advance rigorous interdisciplinary research on the cognitive, psychological, neural, social, and cultural processes involved in religious belief, experience, and behavior.

Vision

Our Vision

We aim to build a durable research network in which scholars across institutions, disciplines, and traditions can develop strong projects, share expertise, and produce work that no single discipline or research group could accomplish alone.

What We Do

What We Do

RCC develops research questions, assembles project teams, supports grant development, coordinates research activity, and helps move projects from initial concept through analysis, publication, and public communication.

  • Develop interdisciplinary research projects.
  • Connect investigators with complementary expertise.
  • Support grant development and research administration.
  • Coordinate project planning, data work, and scholarly outputs.
  • Share findings through publications, presentations, resources, and public scholarship.
Our approach

Rigorous inquiry

We prioritize clear research questions, appropriate methods, transparent reasoning, and conclusions that remain proportionate to the evidence.

Interdisciplinary integration

Religious cognition operates across multiple levels of explanation. Our projects may draw on cognitive science, psychology, neuroscience, philosophy, religious studies, anthropology, and related fields when those perspectives are relevant to the question.

Religious and philosophical openness

RCC is nonconfessional. It does not promote or privilege any religious tradition. We welcome researchers across religious, philosophical, and disciplinary backgrounds and approach religion as a multidimensional human phenomenon worthy of careful study.

Collaborative accountability

Projects are organized around defined roles, responsibilities, timelines, and outputs. Credit and leadership should reflect actual contributions to the work.

Ethical research

Research involving human participants must be conducted under appropriate ethical review and in accordance with applicable institutional, legal, and professional standards.
