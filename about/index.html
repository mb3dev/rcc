About the Collaborative

The Religious Cognition Collaborative is an interdisciplinary research development and operations organization. RCC identifies promising questions, assembles project-specific teams, develops funding strategies, and provides the coordination required to carry research from initial concept through analysis, publication, and public dissemination.

Our mission

Our mission is to advance rigorous interdisciplinary research on religion, cognition, neuroscience, consciousness, and culture by creating the teams and operational structures needed to pursue difficult questions.

What RCC does

RCC develops research projects rather than operating as a conventional laboratory organized around one investigator or method. Projects may begin with a theoretical question, a public dataset, a funding opportunity, an undeveloped collaboration, or a methodological problem. RCC helps define the project, assemble relevant expertise, establish roles, pursue support, coordinate execution, and preserve the resulting publications and resources.

How we work

Questions before methods

Each project begins with a clearly defined problem and uses methods appropriate to that problem.

Expertise matched to the project

Specialized scientific, clinical, computational, and humanistic work is led by personnel with relevant expertise.

Claims proportionate to evidence

RCC distinguishes exploratory, confirmatory, and causal claims and avoids conclusions that exceed the design.

Intellectual and religious openness

RCC is nonconfessional and welcomes researchers across religious, philosophical, methodological, and disciplinary backgrounds.
