About the Collaborative

The Religious Cognition Collaborative develops interdisciplinary research on religion as a cognitive, psychological, neural, social, and cultural phenomenon. Its current work spans secondary analyses of open neuroimaging and pharmacological data alongside a proposed neuroimaging study, and it connects investigators across fields.

Why RCC exists

Why RCC exists

Religious cognition crosses conventional disciplinary boundaries. Questions about belief, experience, ritual, agency, development, and culture often require more than one method and more than one kind of expertise. RCC exists to give those questions an organizational home and to make sustained collaboration easier.

How we work

How we work

Some projects begin within RCC. Others begin with a collaborator, an open dataset, a funding opportunity, or a problem that requires a broader team. RCC helps refine the question, define roles, identify the necessary expertise, coordinate the work, and move the project toward a concrete output.

Open inquiry

Open inquiry across traditions

RCC is nonconfessional. We study religion without presuming either its truth or falsity, and we welcome researchers from religious, nonreligious, and philosophically diverse backgrounds. No single religious tradition is treated as the default model of religion when broader comparison is possible.

Organizational home

Organizational and fiscal home

Religious Cognition Collaborative is the registered trade name (a DBA) of True Dreams Project Fund, a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) organization. True Dreams Project Fund provides fiscal and administrative oversight for RCC's research, including grants, contributions, and compliance, while RCC directs its own research agenda in consultation with the investigators responsible for each project.
