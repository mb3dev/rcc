Current research

RCC develops focused projects with defined questions, methods, roles, and outputs. Our current work spans proposed primary research and secondary analysis of existing neuroimaging datasets.

Religious Socialization and Neural Organization

A large-scale secondary analysis testing whether religious upbringing, current religiosity, and religious importance are associated with brain morphology or shared neural responses during naturalistic viewing.

What we study

Belief and Representation

How people form, maintain, and revise concepts of supernatural agents, sacred realities, religious identities, and moral order.

Experience and Consciousness

How religious, spiritual, mystical, and revelatory experiences arise, are interpreted, and influence thought and behavior.

Brain, Behavior, and Development

How neural, psychological, social, and cultural processes shape religious cognition across time.
