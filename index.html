Religious Cognition Collaborative

Advancing the study of religion and the mind.

We bring researchers together to investigate how religious beliefs, experiences, practices, and representations emerge, function, and change.

Explore Our Research About the Collaborative

Religion is a complex cognitive phenomenon.

Religious cognition draws on perception, memory, emotion, social understanding, embodiment, imagination, and culture. No single discipline can explain it adequately. The Religious Cognition Collaborative develops and supports projects that integrate complementary theories, methods, and forms of expertise.

What we study
Religious Belief

How people represent supernatural agents, sacred realities, doctrines, religious identities, and other religious concepts.

Religious Experience

How religious and spiritual experiences arise, are interpreted, and influence thought, emotion, and behavior.

Brain and Behavior

How cognitive and neural systems contribute to religious belief, practice, experience, and decision-making.

Development and Transmission

How religious cognition is learned, communicated, revised, and expressed across communities and the lifespan.

Research organized around the question.

RCC forms project teams around clearly defined research questions. Each project brings together the investigators, methods, and institutional resources needed for that question, while the Collaborative provides continuity, coordination, and an organizational home.
