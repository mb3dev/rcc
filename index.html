Religious Socialization and Neural Organization

A large-scale secondary analysis examining whether religious upbringing, current religiosity, and religious importance are associated with brain morphology and shared neural responses during naturalistic viewing.

Methods and adjacent research

Alongside its religious-cognition projects, RCC pursues methodological and adjacent analyses that inform that work.

Psilocybin and Default Mode Network Connectivity

A within-subject secondary analysis of psilocybin’s acute effect on default mode network connectivity. It is relevant to RCC through that network’s role in self-referential processing and theories of self-transcendence; it does not itself test a religious variable.
