Current research

Active Research

Religious Socialization and Neural Organization

A large-scale secondary analysis examining whether religious upbringing, current religiosity, and religious importance are associated with brain morphology and shared neural responses during naturalistic viewing.

Secondary data analysis · Structural and functional neuroimaging
View Project
Active Research

Psilocybin and Default Mode Network Connectivity

A within-subject secondary analysis testing whether oral psilocybin acutely reduces internal default mode network connectivity, and whether an eight-week mindfulness program alters that effect.

Secondary data analysis · Resting-state fMRI
View Project
Funding Decision Pending

Adaptive Intelligence: A Dense Longitudinal Neuroimaging Study of Christian Faith-Driven, Self-Directed Neuroplasticity in Parkinson’s Disease Neurodegeneration

A proposed precision-mapping study examining how sustained Christian healing expectation and scriptural prayer relate to within-person changes in brain function and structure among people with Parkinson’s disease.

Proposed longitudinal neuroimaging study · Implementation contingent upon funding

Research areas

Religion, Cognition, and Experience

Research on religious belief, spiritual experience, dreams, ritual, supernatural concepts, and the cognitive processes involved in religious life.

Computational and Neurobehavioral Research

Secondary-data analysis, neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, network analysis, quantitative modeling, and methodological development.

Religion, Narrative, and Culture

Interdisciplinary and quantitative research on religion in literature, media, mythology, intellectual history, and cultural life.
