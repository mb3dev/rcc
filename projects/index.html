Projects

RCC projects are defined by a focused question, a designated leadership structure, an appropriate methodological plan, and a clear path to an output. The status shown on each project indicates whether the work is proposed, active, under analysis, or in publication.

Faith, Healing Expectation, and Neuroplasticity in Parkinson’s Disease

A proposed precision-mapping study examining whether sustained Christian healing expectation is associated with within-person changes in neural function and corticostriatal connectivity.

18 months · 4 participants · Longitudinal multimodal MRI

ANALYSIS COMPLETE

Religious Socialization and Neural Organization

A large-scale secondary analysis testing whether religious upbringing, current religiosity, and religious importance are associated with brain morphology or shared neural responses during naturalistic viewing.

AOMIC ID1000 · N = 925 structural · N = 881 functional
View project
MANUSCRIPT IN PREPARATION

Sleep Architecture and Daily Sacred Cognition

A secondary analysis examining whether nightly REM timing and sleep-stage organization are associated with next-morning closeness to God within persons.

Ambulatory EEG · 72 participants · Approximately 700 nightly observations
View project

Developing a project?

RCC considers projects that fit its research scope and benefit from interdisciplinary coordination. Proposals should begin with a clear question, a realistic path to data or participants, and a preliminary account of the expertise required.
