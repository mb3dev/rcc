Research

Religion cannot be explained at only one level.

RCC studies religion through questions that may require cognitive, psychological, neural, social, cultural, or conceptual explanation. Projects may be empirical, computational, qualitative, or theoretical, depending on the problem being studied.

Religious Belief and Representation

We study how people represent supernatural agents, sacred realities, religious identities, doctrines, moral order, and divine action, and how those representations are maintained or revised.

Religious Experience and Consciousness

We investigate experiences interpreted as religious, spiritual, mystical, revelatory, or supernatural, including their phenomenology, cognitive structure, context, and consequences.

Brain, Behavior, and Embodiment

We examine how distributed neural systems, bodily states, attention, memory, emotion, and action contribute to religious belief, practice, expectation, and experience.

Development, Culture, and Transmission

We study how religious concepts and practices are learned, communicated, embodied, revised, and transmitted across development, communities, narratives, rituals, and media.

Methods

Methods we use

RCC projects use neuroimaging, EEG and sleep measurement, behavioral experiments, psychometrics, intensive longitudinal designs, secondary data analysis, computational modeling, qualitative analysis, and conceptual research, chosen and combined to fit the question at hand.
