Research

RCC organizes its work across three research domains.

Religion, Cognition, and Experience

RCC investigates religious belief, spiritual experience, dreams, ritual, and the cognitive processes involved in religious life.

Computational and Neurobehavioral Research

RCC develops secondary-data, neuroimaging, and computational projects examining cognition, brain dynamics, and behavior. Current work includes network neuroscience, longitudinal analysis, precision mapping, and naturalistic paradigms.

Religion, Narrative, and Culture

RCC supports interdisciplinary and quantitative research on religion in literature, media, mythology, intellectual history, and cultural life.

The Religious Cognition
Collaborative

The Religious Cognition Collaborative develops interdisciplinary research on religion, cognition, neuroscience, consciousness, and culture.

The Religious Cognition Collaborative is a research initiative of True Dreams Project Fund, a U.S. 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization.
